About

Avenhade is the project of a Belgian composer whose work has long been shaped by emotion rather than ego. With roots in progressive rock, cinematic soundscapes, orchestral drama, ambient, and electronic music, 8th Exit is the result of 10 years of introspection, passion, and pain.

In a world of fleeting moments and broken reflections, 8th Exit emerges as the ghostly echo of an intimate journey. Born from the silent spaces between love, loss, and longing, this album is not just music, it’s a mirror to the soul. It’s for those who feel too much. The kind that lingers in your chest like an unanswered question. Music about highways of love and lost time. About a broken ice floe reflecting forgotten memories. The eighth exit on a lonely road leading nowhere but inside yourself.

Each track is a chapter in a story written without words, yet more profound than any spoken language.

8th Exit is not just an album, it’s the sound of someone who refused to forget. Lost in an endless loop he couldn’t escape.

It’s a tribute to a feeling that doesn’t go away, to the person for whom every note was written, and who will never hear them. It’s just a man trying to explain love with music. Again. And again. Eight times. With orchestras, guitars, and a broken heart.

It was written for someone, a girl who loved music and saw something in me I didn’t always see myself... Inspired by the sounds she adored: Starset, Ivan Torrent, Lindsey Stirling, Within Temptation… But also by everything I’ve ever tried to say with music but couldn’t.

8th Exit is flawed like memory itself, but if you listen with care, maybe you’ll feel the shape of someone I never forgot to love.

I once tried to make music under another name: LaTruelleLMDS. It was more than just a project; it was the sound of her, the shape she gave my silence. For years I couldn’t let go of that name because every track felt like an echo of something left unsaid between us. Finally, I deleted it. Not out of regret, but to finally release what had held me back for so long.

If she ever hears this…
I hope it makes sense.

There is no grand story. Only a sound. A feeling.
